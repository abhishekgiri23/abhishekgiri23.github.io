Parental Leave Entitlements

Statutory regulations under the Work and Care Act (WAZO) administered by UWV.

Overview

In the Netherlands, leave associated with the birth, adoption, or fostering of a child is governed by the Work and Care Act (WAZO) and administered by the Employee Insurance Agency (UWV). This guide outlines the statutory minimums provided to the public.

1. Maternity Leave

Mothers employed or receiving unemployment benefits are entitled to a minimum of 16 weeks of leave.

  • 🤰Pregnancy Leave: Must start between 6 and 4 weeks before the expected due date.
  • 👶Maternity Leave: A minimum of 10 weeks must be taken from the day after childbirth.
  • 💰Payment: UWV pays 100% of the daily wage, up to the statutory maximum.
  • 👯Multiple Births: Entitled to at least 20 weeks of leave.

2. Partner Leave (Geboorteverlof)

Standard Partner Leave

1 working week of leave to be taken within 4 weeks after the birth. Paid at 100% by the employer.

Additional Partner Leave

Up to 5 working weeks to be taken within 6 months after the birth.

Payment: Paid by UWV at 70% of the daily wage (up to the capped maximum).

3. Parental Leave (Ouderschapsverlof)

Both parents are entitled to leave for children up to the age of 8. Entitlement: 26 times the weekly working hours.

9 Weeks Paid

Paid by UWV at 70% of daily wage. Must be taken in the first year of child's life.

17 Weeks Unpaid

Can be taken at any time until the child turns 8.

4. Important Regulatory Notes

Maximum Daily Wage

UWV caps all 70% benefits at the "Maximum Daily Wage." High earners may experience a drop in income greater than 30% if their employer does not provide a supplement.

Application

Employees generally apply through their employer. Employers then route the claim to the UWV. Always notify your employer well in advance (minimum 4 weeks for most optional leaves).
